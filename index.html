General introduction about BTS:

BTS (Bangtan Sonyeondan) is a famous South Korean boy band formed by BigHit Entertainment in 2013. With 7 members: RM, Jin, Suga, J-Hope, Jimin, V, and Jungkook, the group has become a global icon in the music industry. BTS stands out not only for their diverse music but also for their powerful messages about love, freedom, and social change.

Notable albums of BTS:

  • Wings (2016): An album with diverse styles, including famous songs like "Blood Sweat & Tears" and "Fire".
  • Love Yourself: Tear (2018): A highly acclaimed album, featuring songs like "Singularity" and "Fake Love".
  • Map of the Soul: 7 (2020): An album containing signature songs like "On" and "Black Swan".
  • BE (2020): An inspiring album created during the pandemic, including "Dynamite" and "Life Goes On".

Famous songs of BTS:

  • "Dynamite": A song that reached high positions on the Billboard Hot 100, it is the first song by BTS to receive a Grammy certification.
  • "Butter": A lively pop song loved globally and broke many music records.
  • "Boy With Luv": Featuring Halsey, this song became one of the standout tracks in the group's career.
  • "Permission to Dance": A positive and accessible song, conveying messages of love and freedom.

Notable achievements of BTS:

  • Received numerous international awards, including Billboard Music Awards and American Music Awards.
  • Notable albums: Wings, Love Yourself: Tear, Map of the Soul: 7, BE.
  • Achieved success with famous songs like "Dynamite", "Butter", "Boy With Luv", and "Permission to Dance".
  • Nominated for Grammy in major categories.
  • Recognized as a global artist with a profound cultural impact.
